On September 11th, 2001, the United States experienced one of the most devastating terrorist attacks in modern history. Four planes were hijacked, two struck the World Trade Center in New York City, one struck the Pentagon in Virginia, while the final plane crashed in Pennsylvania. In New York City 2,753 lives were lost, 184 died at the Pentagon, and all 40 passengers and crew of Flight 93 that crashed in Pennsylvania were lost. The attacks reshaped American culture, politics, religion, philosophy, literature, and artistic expression in ways that continue to influence society today. This presentation will examine the cultural significance of 9/11 through the multiple academic disciplines in order to understand how this singular event changed both the United States and the world.
