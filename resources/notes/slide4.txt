A significant religious consequence of the 9/11 attacks was the modern debate on the connections to religion and violence. The motivations claimed by the attackers were religious in nature and being Muslim, many Americans associated Islam with terrorism. This persisted despite the condemnation of the attacks by the majority of the global Muslim community. The years following 9/11 the Muslim communities across the United States experienced intensified discrimination, surveillance, and suspicion. This demonstrated how a major historical event can influence the perceptions of an entire religious group in disregard of the facts of that community. As noted by Morgan public discussion often relied on incomplete or inaccurate understanding of Islam. This misinformation largely contributed to stereotypes that linked Islam with violence rather than distinguishing between a peaceful global religious majority and the violent extremist organizations of the minority. Another consequence of the attacks being linked to Islamic extremism is that the violence associated with other religions was viewed differently. The violence associated with Christianity and other religions were minimized or denied, while Islam was portrayed as a religion rooted in violence. This reveals how 9/11 influenced attitudes toward terrorism but also the broader cultural discussions of religion.
