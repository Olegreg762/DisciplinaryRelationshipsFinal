Literature became an important avenue for Americans to process the trauma of the attacks and understand the new rapidly changing world. Post-9/11 fiction according to DeRosa often grappled with loss and national identity. Authors explored how the attacks affected people and reshaped our culture rather than simply recounting the attacks. Literature also became an important medium to understanding the motivations behind terrorism. The analysis of Don DeLillo’s Falling Man and John Updike’s Terrorist by Eikonsalo demonstrates how authors attempted to understand motivations behind terrorist violence and explored the psychological and social consequences of terrorism. Readers of these novels were challenged to confront difficult questions about fear, identity, and cultural conflict. Americans were able to reflect on the meaning of 9/11 through literature and preserve the emotional impacts in ways that strict historical accounts could not achieve alone.
